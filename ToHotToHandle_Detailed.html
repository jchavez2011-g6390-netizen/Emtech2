What is Climate Change?

Climate change describes long-term shifts in temperature and weather patterns. While natural forces can influence climate, the rapid warming observed since the industrial era is primarily driven by human activities that emit greenhouse gases.

Major Causes

  • Burning of fossil fuels for energy and transport
  • Deforestation and land-use change
  • Industrial processes and waste
  • Agricultural emissions (methane, nitrous oxide)

Key Effects

  • Rising sea levels and stronger storms
  • Heatwaves, droughts, and changing rainfall
  • Loss of biodiversity and threats to food security
  • Greater risks for coastal communities

Impact in the Philippines

The Philippines is highly vulnerable to climate impacts. Rising sea levels, stronger typhoons, coastal erosion, and changing rainfall patterns threaten livelihoods, agriculture, and infrastructure. Deforestation and expanding fossil fuel projects have also contributed to environmental pressures in many regions.

Solutions & What Governments Can Do

  • Invest in renewable energy (solar, wind, hydro)
  • Protect and restore forests and wetlands
  • Improve public transport and reduce vehicle emissions
  • Upgrade coastal defenses and early warning systems

Water Pollution — Causes, Impacts, and Solutions

Water pollution occurs when harmful substances — such as chemicals, waste, or plastic — enter rivers, lakes, and oceans. Polluted water damages ecosystems, harms human health, and undermines livelihoods that depend on fisheries and tourism.

Common Causes

  • Industrial discharge (untreated or poorly treated wastewater)
  • Urban runoff carrying oil, trash, and chemicals
  • Sewage and inadequate sanitation systems
  • Agricultural runoff (fertilizers, pesticides)
  • Plastic pollution and ghost fishing gear

Direct Impacts

  • Harm to marine life and biodiversity loss
  • Contaminated drinking water and disease risk
  • Reduced fish stocks and damaged fisheries
  • Loss of tourism revenue in coastal areas

How It Affects the Philippines

Many Philippine coastlines and waterways face pollution from urban centers, shipping, and industrial activities. Local communities relying on fishing are particularly affected. Community-based cleanup efforts and stronger waste management can make a big difference.

What You Can Do

  • Reduce single-use plastics and participate in beach/riverside cleanups
  • Properly dispose of hazardous household waste
  • Support local policies for better sewage and industrial wastewater treatment
  • Report illegal dumping and support sustainable fishing practices

Case Study: Everyday Sources

A typical city storm drains surface runoff directly into rivers; this runoff often carries oil from vehicles, litter, and chemicals from homes and yards. In rural areas, agricultural runoff can carry fertilizers into waterways, causing algal blooms that suffocate fish.

Action Checklist — How to Help Today

At Home

  • Use reusable bags and bottles
  • Fix leaks and conserve water
  • Compost organic waste where possible

Community

  • Join local cleanups and tree planting
  • Organize awareness drives in schools
  • Report pollution and illegal dumping

Policy

  • Vote for leaders with clear climate plans
  • Support local waste-management initiatives
  • Advocate for better coastal protection
